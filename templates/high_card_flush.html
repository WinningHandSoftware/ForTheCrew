High Card Flush - Tips & Tricks

1. Opening & Shuffling Procedures

  • Inspect and count both decks before opening the table.
  • Change decks every 4 hours or when necessary.
  • If the automatic shuffler fails, use the backup or perform a manual shuffle.

2. Dealing & Game Flow

  • Players must place an Ante bet to play.
  • Deal 7 cards face down to each player.
  • Encourage players to check their hands before folding—folding a 4-card Flush is rare.

3. Key Rules to Remember

  • Dealer needs at least a 9-high, 3-card Flush to qualify.
  • If the dealer does not qualify, Ante bets are paid even, and Raise bets push.
  • If the dealer qualifies, compare hands:
    • More suited cards = higher rank.
    • If tied, use the highest card in the Flush.
    • Continue comparing cards until a winner is determined.

4. Handling Payouts

  • Ante & Raise pay 1:1 if the player wins.
  • Side Bets (Flush & Straight Flush Bonus) pay based on a set table.
  • Highest payout: 8000-1 for a 7-card Straight Flush.
  • Pay or take bets from right to left, in this order: Raise > Ante > Side Bets.

5. Managing Tips (Tokes)

  • Players must bet an Ante to place a Raise bet for the dealer.
  • No soliciting tips—strictly prohibited.
  • All tip bets must be placed in the toke box after being shown to Surveillance.

6. Common Irregularities & Disputes

  • Misdeal: Incorrect card distribution results in a voided hand and reshuffle.
  • Face-up card during deal: Reshuffle the deck.
  • Shuffler malfunction: Stop play & notify the supervisor.
