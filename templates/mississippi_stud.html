Mississippi Stud - Tips & Tricks

1. Game Basics

  • Mississippi Stud is a five-card poker game played against a paytable.
  • Each player is dealt two cards and combines them with three community cards.
  • Players must place an Ante bet to receive their cards.

2. Betting Structure

  • Ante: Required to play.
  • 3rd, 4th, and 5th Street Bets: Players can fold or raise (1x, 2x, or 3x their Ante).
  • Side Bets: 3 Card Bonus & Progressive Jackpot.

3. Hand Rankings (Best to Worst)

  • Royal Flush – A-K-Q-J-10 (same suit)
  • Straight Flush – Five consecutive suited cards
  • Four of a Kind – Four cards of the same rank
  • Full House – Three of a kind + a pair
  • Flush – Five cards of the same suit
  • Straight – Five consecutive non-suited cards
  • Three of a Kind
  • Two Pair
  • One Pair (Jacks or Better)
  • Pair of 6s-10s Pushes
  • High Card (No Pair) – Loses

4. Dealing Procedure

  • Players place their Ante and optional side bets before cards are dealt.
  • The dealer distributes two cards per player and three community cards face-down.
  • Players decide whether to fold or bet after each community card is revealed.
  • After the final community card, payouts are determined based on the paytable.

5. Payouts

  • Winning hands are paid based on the paytable (e.g., Royal Flush = 500 to 1).
  • Players with Jacks or better win; Pair of 6s-10s push; anything less loses.
  • The 3 Card Bonus is paid based only on the three community cards.

6. Handling Tips (Tokes)

  • Players must place an Ante to bet a Toke for the dealer.
  • Toke pays the same odds as the player's bet.
  • No soliciting tips.
  • Winning tips must be placed directly into the toke box.
  • Maximum Toke: 10% of the table max if the player bets the table max.

7. Common Irregularities & Disputes

  • Misdeal = Reshuffle & restart.
  • Under-minimum bet noticed late = Play proceeds; dealer informs player.
  • Over-table max bet = Only the max is paid/taken.
  • Talking or showing hands = Warning first; further violations void hand.
